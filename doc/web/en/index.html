What is Suika2?

Suika2 is a world leading tool targeted towards visual novel creation in both the amateur and professional space. Built to run on various devices, including Windows PC, Mac, iPhone, iPad, Android, Linux, game consoles, and more, you can easily produce impressive visual novels without sacrificing the joy of creation.

The Benefits

  • Suika2 comes with lightweight yet powerful development tools that run on Windows PC, Mac, iPhone and iPad.
  • Suika2 is ideal for an introduction to indie game development.
  • You can create multilingualized games.
  • You can export a game app for iPhone or Android by a single click
  • You can publish your games to commercial stores for monetization and it doesn't cost fees at all for engine usage.
  • You can shift from an indie production to a commercial production smoothly.
  • Suika2 is released under the MIT license and you don't have to worry about engine costs.
  • Just following our easy tutorial makes you up!
  • Full-scale development tools supports from debugging to publishing for high work efficiency.
  • Light and stable operation for players; A great work should have a great feel!

Suika2's Vision, Mission, and Values

  • Simple: Anyone can easily create visual novels.

Where can I use Suika2

Thanks to Suika2 HAL-based porting system, you can use it on almost any platform.

For example, Suika2 is availble as:

  • iPhone and iPad apps
  • Android apps
  • Windows apps (with or without installer)
  • Mac apps
  • Web browser apps

Recommended for Developers...

  • who want to create visual novels and publish them as free games
  • who are looking to eventually publish them to app stores
  • who want to run games on their own smartphone/tablet (no store review)
  • who want to publish games on Googla Play, App Store, Steam, DLsite, and more (requires store review)

The History of Suika2

  • December 2001: Suika Studio (Studio) initial release
  • 2002 to 2003: Studio is distributed via CD-ROM in magazines
  • Mid 2004: Studio's final release goes live
  • August 2016: Suika2 initial release
  • April 2022: Suika2 Pro initial released
  • August 2022: Suika2 receives GUI support
  • October 2022: Project reorganised on GitHub
  • January 2023: Suika2 Kirara initial release and later deprecation
  • May 2023: Inaugural Suika2 Awards (2023) are held
  • October 2023: Several awarded works are released on the iOS and Android stores
  • December 2023: Suika2 Pro Mobile and Suika2 Pro Desktop are released on major digital stores

The World Suika2 Strives For

In the late 1990s and early 2000s, software such as Kirikiri/KAG and NScripter led the doujin and commercial visual novel markets into a period of strong industry growth; however, after the major gaming platform shift to smartphones in the 2010s, it became apparent that no standard software had been created to run visual novels on smartphones, leaving a major gap within the industry.

This was due to the market's observation that light games would be replaced by HTML5 technology. Thus, many of the visual novel engines that emerged in the 2010s were web technologies; however, the future of light games being HTML5-based didn't come to fruition. There are, of course, hybrid apps that mix web and native technologies, but HTML5 never reached the level of mainstream in terms of game development technology. As a result, until October 2023, there was no standard software that could easily bring visual novels to smartphones. That kind of software was missing.

Suika2 is something that has never existed before. Namely, it is open source, cross-platform (including smartphones), and runs natively, not via the Web or Python. Suika2 is the first visual novel engine with these particular features that can be easily used for both doujin and commercial purposes.

Suika2 encourages the creation of games by individuals and expands the platform on which these works are published, from PC and Web to smartphones.

Suika2 makes it easy for popular works to generate revenue on smartphone app stores. By establishing a scheme that allows unique creators to generate revenue, Suika2 strives to create a small market that will eventually grow into a large industry.

Ultimately, Suika2 envisions a world where just a single computer becomes a great production tool for the disadvantaged. This will break the stagnation that has prevailed in Japan for the better part of 30 years. It will also bring prosperities to developing countries. That is the future we wish to realize.
